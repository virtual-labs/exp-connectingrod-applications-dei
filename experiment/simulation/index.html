Simulation Setup of Step-1

Description: Connecting rod forming process comprises billet compression and deformation by closed impression die forming process. The upper half section of the connecting rod is impressed on the upper die while lower half is on the lower die. The material is manganese alloy steel maintained at 1250°C. A hydraulic press applies high force, causing the material to flow plastically into the die impressions. Preform connecting rod is shown at the end.

Step-1 with Strain

Description: The variation of equivalent strain is shown on the right-hand side of the video during the 1250°C forming process. High deforming force and billet temperature cause the material to move into the impressions created in the upper and lower die. At the final height, the billet is deformed into a semi-finished connecting rod.

Simulation Setup of Step-2

Description: Maintaining a temperature of 1250°C, the upper die compresses the billet with high force. Material fills the impressions to form the semi-finished connecting rod. Right hand side shows initial, pre-formed, and final billet transition. Final shape is obtained after heat treatment and machining.

Step-2 with Strain

Description: This simulation displays the final equivalent strain distribution. The manganese alloy steel flows into the die cavities to ensure a dense structure. The result is a semi-finished connecting rod that possesses high fatigue resistance and strength after machining.
